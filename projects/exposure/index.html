Challenge

This project was created for the University of Toronto on the occasion of a Colloquium. The subject was really unique, so my first thoughts were to make this poster really stand out. Silly me. This is an academic institution! The challenge was to convey information while at the same time adhering to the academic voice, reputation, and visual standards of an institution of the calibre such as the University of Toronto.

Solution

Simplicity, my friend. I created a fluid visual path for the viewer. One is able to retrieve information from the poster, and move on. User experience here was key. By assuming that students would be in a rush while viewing this poster, I isolated the most important elements of the poster, such as the date and location. By adding simple lines, the eye immediately focuses on the latter. I used an Isotype to draw the semiotic connection between subject matter and art.

Fun Fact: This poster was printed over 1000 times! Pretty darn awesome.
