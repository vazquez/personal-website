Dripless: A Water Conservation Movement

View More Screenshots
website

Challenge

Canadians rank second only to the United States in terms of highest per capita water use in the developed world. Canadians consume about 1.5 million cubic metres or approximately 4,400 litres-per-capita-per-day. The amount of fresh water needed for human survival is approximately 5 litres-percapita-per-day. That doesn't sound like rainbows and butterflies to me.

Solution

Converse. Dialogue, whether visual or verbal, always has the potential to be remembered by its listener. By integrating interactive elements on the website, I invited the user to be immersed in the experience. Now that is something worth talking about.


This website is not completed yet. In the near future, the user will be able to receive a score based on their survey answers. I just have to write the algorithm, yay! But, were friends, you can see the work in progress. Send me feedback if you'd like!
Fun Fact: This idea was conceived when I realized how much water I was wasting. My bad.
