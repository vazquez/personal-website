1095 Days: Typographic Exercise

View Project
print

I created 1095 Days to visualize the ephemeral: romantic emotion. As I neared the 3rd anniversary of my partner and I, I wanted to create an original piece that showcased, through typography, the key paradigms of a strong relationship. Aiding myself with Isotypes, I was able to create an intimate

and constantly evolving poster. As with any relationship that is rewarded with longevity, emotional possibilities are endless. This poster allows the reader to create and re-create word puzzles, coming up with a different sentence every time one follows a new visual path.

Fun Fact: I was nominated for the Adobe Design Achievement Award - Print Design (2011), all thanks to this lovely piece of art.
